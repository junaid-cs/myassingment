Why Is It Necessary for Students to Cite in Vancouver Style?

Including text citations and a referencing index in the correct style (featuring the year published, cited date, and details of journal article/books and websites) is mandatory for academic writing. One can also consider it as a vital grading parameter. Below are more reasons why you (as a student) must use the Vancouver citation style in your assignment papers.

To Add Credibility - Citing texts and sources in the specified style adds credibility to your academic paper, making it appear professional and up to the college/university's standards. Use our Vancouver citation generator to simplify in-text citations and the referencing process in an acceptable style.

Give Authority to the Original Author - You use the Vancouver citation style to acknowledge and give authority to the original author, especially when using multiple sources. Moreover, you can also refer to the in-text citations and referenced sources in the bibliography if you need more information.

Helps Readers Locate Sources - Including Vancouver referencing also lets readers locate sources to learn more about whatever you have covered in the paper. It also helps them understand the context when reading your work.

Who Uses the Vancouver Citation Style?

Scientific, Medical, and Health Care students use the Vancouver citation style in vivid scientific publications and medical/health-related journal article to cite and create a reference list/bibliography. Using the citation style, pupils can reference numbers and cite examples either using superscripts or in line with the text using brackets.  

Of course, it must appear in the order or citation number as they appear. Also, when referencing cites multiple times, give the same number.

Published Example(s) of How to Cite in Vancouver Citation Style

In the Vancouver style, the reference list should be published in numerical order. Here is the format - Author Name. Name of Book/Journal articles, etc. Publisher. Year Published (URL Cited Date Accessed for online sources)

  • Wilkinson IB, Raine T. Wiles K, Goodhart A, Hall C, O’Neill H. Oxford Handbook of Clinical Medicine, 10th ed. Oxford: Oxford University Press; 2017.
  • Darden L., Mechanisms and models. In: Hull DI., Ruse M, editors. The Cambridge companion to the philosophy of biology. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press; 2008 p. 139-159.
  • Cancer Research UK. Researching into breast cancer [Internet]. 2022 [cited 2022 Jun 28]. Available from:http://www.cancerresearchuk.org/our-research-by-cancer-type/our-research-into-breast-cancer
  • Breast cancer [Internet]. 2022 [cited 2022 Jun 28]. Available from: http//wikipedia.org/wiki/Breast_cancer

What Does the Vancouver Citation Style Look Like?

The texts to be cited must be in ascending order beginning from number 1, written in either brackets or superscripts. These numbers (numerically ordered) must then be included in a reference list containing all the information in cited sources.
