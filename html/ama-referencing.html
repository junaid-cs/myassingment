How To Do AMA In-Text Citations In AMA Citation Style?

The in-text citations in AMA style, not to be confused with AMA in-text citations, use a number in superscript where the source seems relevant in the text. The numbers used in the superscript need to be in chronological order from the beginning of the research paper to the end. Usually, a citation is mentioned with a quote or at the end of the sentence where the source has contributed.

Example: The drug is used to treat malaria.1

The superscript number in each citation within the text leads the reader to the complete reference in the AMA reference list at the end of the paper. It is perhaps the simplest in-text citation format that you may need to work with during your academic career. If you want, you can use our CSE citation generator to create a detailed reference for each citation with perfection.

Example: Keeping the drug in room temperature can reduce its potency.3,4

If you are citing more than one reference at the same point, you need to separate the superscript numbers with commas and no spaces between. When more than two references are cited, at a given place in the text, use hyphens to join the first and last numbers of a closed series. You should use commas without space to separate other parts of multiple citations. you can also take help from our latest edition AMA in referencing generator.

Example: The subject did not respond to any of the drugs that other researchers suggested.5-8

Use AMA Referencing Generator & Create A Reference List In AMA Format

Once you are done citing the in-text references, you need to provide the complete detail of the reference in the AMA reference list. You can also use the AMA reference generator at zero cost from our website to get perfect solutions. However, if you want to do it on your own, you need to remember the following guidelines:

  • For printed articles:

While writing the reference details of an article, use this following template:

Author AA. Title of the article. Abbreviated Title of Journal. Year of publication;vol(issue):pages.

  • For printed books:

While writing the reference details of AMA book citation, use this following template:

Author AA. Title of Book. Edition number (if applicable). City, state of publication: publisher’s name; year of publication.

  • For websites:

To write the details of the AMA citation of a website, use this template:

Author(s) (if applicable). Title of the specific item cited (if no such title is given, use the name of the organization responsible for the site). Name of the website. URL. Published [date]. Updated [date]. Accessed [date]. (If a published or updated date is not given, either write "published/updated date not given" or omit this field).

  • For non-scholarly materials appearing in a journal:

To write the reference details of non-scholarly materials such as editorials, letters to the editor, comments and interviews, etc., you need to use the following template:

Author AA. Title of article [type of the article]. Abbreviated Title of Journal. Year of publication;vol(issue):pages.

      • Certain guidelines applicable to all sources:
      • AMA allows the citation of multiple authors (up to 6) in a reference entry. All the names of the authors (1-6) should be mentioned in the same format: Surname initials of the first name. Example: Warner DB
      • If the source is written by 7 or more authors, list the first three names, and then abbreviate with et al. Example: Warner DB, Cole S, Turner KL, et al.
      • The titles of the journals need to be abbreviated. The abbreviation of journal titles can be found in an NLM Catalog. If you cannot find the abbreviation of a particular journal, consult section 14.10 of the AMA Manual of Style for standard abbreviations.
      • The capitalization of titles in references follows the original capitalization of the publication. However, if the original capitalization is not available, capitalize all the major words in the title. If “the”, “a”, “an”, “in”, “for”, or “but” comes at the beginning of the title, you should capitalize the first letter; otherwise, they are not capitalized. The acronyms of the title should be shown in all caps. However, for book chapters and journal articles, only the first word is capitalized.
      • If the name of the author is missing, use the existing guidelines for AMA references and omit the author's name.

      AMA Formatting Rules - Need To Know Every Thing

      You need to follow the AMA formatting rules while drafting your paper. So, keep these following guidelines in mind:

      • Margins:

      You need to leave one-inch margins around the text. Leave the right margin unjustified. You don't need to use columns here.

      • Line spacing:

      The main text of the paper must be double-spaced. Other parts of the paper, including the abstract, notes, tables, figures, references, titles and headings, etc., should be single-spaced.

      • Font:

      The ideal font for AMA formatting style is the Times New Roman, 12-point font.

      • Indents:

      You need to use half-inch indents for new paragraphs. You can maintain that by using the “tab” button on your keyboard.

      • Page numbers:

      The page numbers should be placed at the top-right corner of the page. You need to start marking the page number from the title page.

      • Page headers:

      The title of the document should be placed in the top-left corner of the page.

      • Title page:

      If your paper needs a title page, you need to write the following details on the center of the first blank page of the document:

      • Title of document
      • Your name
      • Your Instructor’s name
      • Course title
      • Due date

      If the paper does not require a title page, just write the details (except the title of the document) in the top-right corner of the first page.

      Frequently Asked Questions


      What Is AMA Style In EndNote?

      In American Medical Association (AMA) style, endnotes are used to cite paraphrased or quoted context in the main text. Users have to mention the endnote sources using superscript.

       Also, every superscript number should be hyperlinked and once clicked, should take the user to the mentioned source in the endnote.

      Who Uses The AMA Style?

      The AMA format is used for citing sources in medical academic tasks.

       Of you are writing assignments related to medical disciplines and others related to it, use the AMA manual to cite your tasks. The American Medical Association creates this guide. Currently, the 10th Edition of AMA is being approved in academic works.

      Is AMA Citation In Alphabetical Order?

      No, AMA citation style does not follow alphabetical order.

      Instead, to create in-text citations in the AMA style, the user needs to include a number in superscript where the source has been cited in the text. Often, an AMA citation is shown with a quote or at the end of the sentence where the source has contributed. The AMA style requires that a reference list in numerical order to reflect the order of in-text citations. This means the reference list is never presented in alphabetical order.

      Is AMA The Same As Jama?

      Yes, the AMA format is often used interchangeably as JAMA. It is widely used to cite academic works in medical and its related disciplines. Currently, the American Medical Association has taken information from AMA Manual of Style (10th Edition).

      The JAMA style will need:

      • Use of initials of the authors' names.
      • All author (s) names are listed if less than 6. If more than 6, one has to list the first three followed by et al. for the remaining names.
