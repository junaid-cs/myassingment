Frequently Asked Questions


How do I use the Oscola referencing style?

To use the Oscola citation style, you need to follow specific guidelines. There are different rules for writing different types of paper. For writing on legal authorities, you need to include formatting citations in footnotes. You can also include in-text citations and provide a bibliography at the end of your document.

Is the Oscola referencing style compatible with various citation styles?

Oscola is a distinct referencing style. It is designed specifically for legal writing. So, it may not be compatible with other citation styles like APA or MLA.So, if you are writing a law paper, it is better to use this style.

Is adding a footnote mandatory if you want to cite in Oscola format?

Yes, in the Oscola format, footnotes are mandatory to cite content. In legal writing, footnotes are commonly used to provide references. They are also used to add additional explanations like the name of the publisher or the publication. However, Oscola does add some flexibility in citation styles within footnotes. Generally, footnotes should contain the citation details of the sources referenced in the text.

Is it important to mention publisher details while citing a website in Oscola style?

When citing a website in OSCOLA style, writing the publisher or publication details is not a common practice. Instead, you can add the name of the author. Instead of the publisher, you can also add the -

Title of the web page

The URL

The date you accessed the website

Always ensure to check the specific guidelines provided by your publication. 

 

 

What is Oscola referencing?

Oscola is a referencing style primarily used in legal academic writing. This form of reference is used to cite legal sources such as cases, statutes, and treaties. Students, however, use this form of reference to write research papers.
