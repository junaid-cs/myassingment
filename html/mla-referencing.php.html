Despite various editions of the style, there are a few things that are constant. Here’s what a researcher takes into account while crafting a works cited page:

  • Author
  • Source title
  • Container title
  • Other contributors
  • Publication date
  • Page number
  • Access date

An Insight into the Meaning of MLA In-Text Citations

The MLA style is not restricted to acknowledging all sources in the ‘works cited page’ in your assignment. MLA calls for both in-text and MLA works cited page made separately for the sources you have referred to. So, what will you have to do when you are asked to create in-text references? First, the text citations serve as a way to direct readers to the entire works cited entry list. Second, it is mandatory to use such references when you keep a sentence within quotation marks while writing the paper.

A typical text citation must have the last name of the writer followed by the page numbers or only the page number from where you have used the quote. The MLA format for the works cited entries in the case of the Modern Language Association will be different from the others. A Deep Dive into the MLA 9th Edition

The format for MLA citations or other citation styles, such as Harvard or APA, keeps changing from time to time. Presently, students have to follow the 9th edition to create MLA works cited list. So, what is this edition? Let’s understand.

The present edition was introduced in April 2021 with a focus on clarification, guidance, and expansion on MLA 8. The MLA style guides on this edition are directed towards adding finesse and more perfection to each works cited entry. This edition has also taken a deeper dive into the in-text parenthetical citation and the ways to use the English language to share the author and other details properly.. Further, the edition has re-introduced guidance for research in-text citations as well as the format for works cited page for research papers.
